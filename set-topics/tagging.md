*Tagging* - as a method to address targets - is a generic method, and multiple systems use it, multiple ways:

**Case-A**: *tracking objects*
We can tag an object for ninja to track / and you let ninja know about this tag --- so we have a "pair": OBJECT-NINJA --- explained here: https://youtu.be/vXalfRAnXak?t=1283 and here: https://youtu.be/YSMqt0J15bQ?list=PLVCUepYV6TvMXN8HQjLU9wKz-G_6JvJsF&t=567

**Case-B**: *applying materials on objects*
We can tag an object, and make ninja apply a given material on this object - the method is called DIRECT DRIVE, explained here: https://youtu.be/vXalfRAnXak?t=3724

**Case-C**: *making the Landscape Utility Actor (NOT ninja Actor) apply materials on LANDSCAPE objects*
We tag a landscape, we provide the Landscape Utility with the tag, and it applies a material on the landscape.
Explained here: https://youtu.be/UBNjhb4J8I0?list=PLVCUepYV6TvMXN8HQjLU9wKz-G_6JvJsF&t=781

**Case-D**: telling the FloorSnapper Utility Actor what floor objects to IGNORE when looking for floor-normals
